## 2. Services

My event work focuses on documentary-style photography and videography — capturing the atmosphere, people, details, interactions, and key moments of an event so the content can be used for marketing, social media, brand storytelling, internal promotion, press, and future event visibility.

Depending on the Proposal, services may include:

- Event photography
- Event videography
- Highlight reels and short-form social clips
- Host, speaker, chef, performer, founder, or team portraits
- Documentary coverage of the event as it unfolds
- Key moments, speeches, presentations, performances, launches, tastings, demonstrations, or guest interactions
- Venue, atmosphere, styling, food, product, and detail content
- Behind-the-scenes footage
- Guest, speaker, partner, or client testimonial clips, where planned and consented to in advance
- Promotional storytelling content for future events, launches, campaigns, or internal communications

My style is **documentary, cinematic, and unobtrusive**. I capture what naturally happens rather than staging or over-directing the Event. I may offer light direction where useful, especially for portraits, hero shots, product moments, team photographs, chef/founder content, or intentional promotional moments.

These services are especially suited to:

- Corporate events and company gatherings
- Brand activations
- Product launches
- Gastronomic and culinary events
- Chef collaborations and pop-ups
- Hospitality and atmosphere-led events
- Private dining experiences
- Creative, cultural, and community events
- Talks, panels, workshops, and networking events
- Private events and celebrations

The following are **not offered** unless expressly agreed in writing:

- Studio product photography
- E-commerce photography
- Heavily staged commercial production
- Architectural or interior design photography
- Advanced beauty retouching
- Complex visual effects or compositing
- Large-scale agency production services

Only Deliverables listed in the Proposal are included. Additional services, shoot hours, edits, formats, usage rights, or Deliverables requested on the day or after acceptance may be quoted separately.

## 3. Pricing Basis & Booking Retainer

Unless otherwise stated in the Proposal, single-event work is billed at €30.00/hour.

Hourly billing covers the hours actually filmed or photographed. If I am required to remain on-site across a gap in the schedule, that time may be billed as part of the Event coverage. If the Event includes separated active filming blocks and I am free to leave between them, only the active filming blocks are billed.

For Events outside Barcelona, mileage may be charged at €0.26/km, unless another travel arrangement is stated in the Proposal.

A Project is confirmed only once the Client has accepted the Proposal in writing and the required Retainer has been received.

Until both steps are complete, the requested Event date remains available to other clients.

Unless otherwise stated in the Proposal:

- **Repeat clients:** 30% non-refundable Retainer due at booking, with the remaining 70% due according to the payment schedule stated in the Proposal or invoice.
- A repeat client means a client with at least one prior project fully completed and paid.
- Reserved dates not confirmed within **3 business days** may be released.
- Retainers are non-refundable under all circumstances.
- Accepted payment methods may include bank transfer, credit card, Bizum, or cash.
- Invoiced balances are due within the term stated on the invoice, or within **30 calendar days** if no shorter term is stated, subject to the legal maximum described in Section 14.

Any additional Deliverables, rush delivery, extra hours, travel expenses, rentals, crew, subcontractors, or project-specific add-ons must be stated in the Proposal or approved in writing.

- Travel, parking, accommodation, equipment, subcontractor, or crew costs already booked for the Event may be charged.
- Cancellations close to the Event date may carry additional fees if stated in the Proposal.

Rescheduling requires at least **24 hours' notice** and is subject to availability.

If a Project is rescheduled, the existing Retainer may be transferred to the new date at my discretion, provided the new date is available and the rescheduling request is reasonable.

If I need to reschedule due to illness, emergency, technical failure, travel disruption, or another force majeure event, the Project will be rescheduled at no additional creative fee, and any Retainer already paid will remain valid for the new date.

Force majeure includes circumstances beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to illness, injury, severe weather, natural disaster, transport disruption, government restrictions, venue closure, civil unrest, equipment theft, power failure, or other events that make performance unsafe, impossible, or materially impractical.

Weather conditions may require adapting the creative approach. Weather does not constitute a breach of this Agreement.
